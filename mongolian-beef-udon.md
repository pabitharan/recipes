# Mongolian Beef Udon

**Serves:** 4 · **Prep:** 5 min · **Cook:** 10 min · **Marinate:** 30 min

## Ingredients

- 1 lb beef strips (stir-fry strips, or steak cut into strips)
- 1 cup diced green onion, plus more for garnish
- 14 oz shelf-stable udon noodles
- 2 tsp vegetable oil, for frying

### Marinade

- 1/2 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp white sugar
- 1 tbsp cornstarch
- 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil

### Sauce

- 1/2 tsp ground ginger (or 1 tsp fresh, minced)
- 1 tbsp fresh garlic, minced
- 1/2 cup soy sauce
- 1/2 cup water
- 1/3 cup brown sugar
- 1 tbsp cornstarch

### Garnish

- Sliced green onions
- Sesame seeds

## Instructions

1. Whisk marinade ingredients in a medium bowl. Add beef and refrigerate 30 minutes.
2. Whisk sauce ingredients in another bowl and set aside.
3. Heat 2 tsp oil in a wok or skillet over medium-high until very hot. Add beef and cook, stirring, until cooked through.
4. Add green onion and cook, stirring, until softened and slightly browned.
5. Add the sauce and cook, stirring, 1 minute.
6. Add udon, gently loosen the noodles, then cook, stirring, until the sauce thickens and noodles are warmed through.
7. Garnish with green onion and sesame seeds.
