# Vegetable Korma

## Ingredients

### Vegetables

- 1 cup frozen peas
- 1/2 head cauliflower, cut into florets (~3 cups)
- 2 carrots, chopped (~1 cup)
- 4 small Yukon gold potatoes, diced (~2 cups)
- Salt

### Seasoning paste

- 1 tsp mustard seed
- 1 tsp cumin seed
- 2 cloves garlic, peeled
- A knob of fresh ginger, peeled

### Other

- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 green chile, minced (optional)
- 1/4 onion, diced (~1/2 cup)
- 1 roma tomato, diced (~1/2 cup)
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- 1/2 tsp Kashmiri chile powder (or cayenne)
- 1/4 cup plain yogurt
- 2 tbsp coconut cream (or 2/3 cup coconut milk)
- ~1 cup water, to thin
- Salt to taste

### To serve

- Chopped cilantro
- Rice, roti, or dal

## Instructions

1. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add a pinch of salt and the vegetables. Par-cook 3–5 minutes. Drain and set aside.
2. **Seasoning paste.** Grind mustard and cumin seed in a mortar and pestle (or blender). Add garlic and ginger and crush into a paste.
3. Heat oil in a wok or Dutch oven over medium. Add chile, onion, and tomato. Sauté 1–2 minutes, until starting to brown.
4. Add the seasoning paste, chile powder, and turmeric. Cook 1–2 more minutes, stirring.
5. Stir in yogurt and coconut cream. Lower heat to low. Add water and the cooked vegetables.
6. Simmer, stirring occasionally, about 10 minutes, until the sauce thickens slightly.
7. Adjust consistency with more water (thinner) or yogurt/coconut (thicker). Salt to taste.
8. Top with cilantro and serve over rice.
