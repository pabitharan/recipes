# Tonkatsu

**Serves:** 2–4 · **Time:** 45 min

## Ingredients

### Pork cutlets

- 1 lb boneless pork chops (2–4 chops)
- 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
- 3 tbsp cornstarch
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
- Neutral oil, for frying
- Salt

### Ginger garlic slaw

- 1/4 head cabbage, sliced thin
- 1 1/2 tbsp Kewpie mayo
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp Sriracha
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 1/4 carrot, peeled
- A small knob fresh ginger
- 1 clove garlic
- Zest of 1/2 lemon

### To serve

- Tonkatsu sauce
- Lemon wedges

## Instructions

1. **Slaw dressing.** Grate the ginger, garlic, and carrot into a bowl. Add lemon zest, mayo, rice vinegar, soy sauce, and Sriracha. Mix and taste; adjust seasoning. Toss with the cabbage, or save to spoon over the top.
2. **Bread the pork.** Pound or slice the pork to about 3/4-inch thick. Salt both sides.
3. Set up three trays: flour mixed with cornstarch, beaten egg, and panko.
4. Heat about 2 inches of oil in a wok over medium until it reaches 350°F.
5. Coat each cutlet in flour mixture, then egg, then panko, pressing the panko on so it sticks.
6. **Fry.** Lower cutlets into the oil and fry 7–9 minutes, agitating slightly, until golden brown.
7. Drain on a wire rack over paper towel. Sprinkle with salt.
8. Slice and serve with tonkatsu sauce, the cabbage slaw, and lemon wedges.
