# Banh Mi Sandwiches

**Serves:** 2 · **Prep:** 30 min · **Cook:** 1 hr

## Ingredients

### Pork

- 1 lb pork belly, sliced
- 1 clove garlic, sliced
- 2 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 2 tbsp brown sugar
- 2 bird's eye chiles, sliced
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 2 tbsp neutral oil

### Carrot-daikon slaw

- ~1 cup thinly sliced carrot
- ~1 cup thinly sliced daikon radish
- 1 cup rice vinegar
- 1/2 cup water
- 1/2 cup sugar
- 1 tsp salt

### Sandwich

- 2 personal-sized Vietnamese baguettes
- 1/2 cucumber, sliced lengthwise
- 1 bunch cilantro, chopped
- 1 bunch mint, chopped
- 1 jalapeño, sliced into rounds
- 4 tbsp mayonnaise
- 2 tbsp Sriracha or other chile sauce

## Instructions

1. Combine the pork marinade ingredients (everything but the pork) in a glass container. Add pork, cover, and refrigerate at least 2 hours, ideally overnight.
2. In a small saucepan, combine the slaw liquid (vinegar, water, sugar, salt) and bring to a gentle boil, stirring until sugar dissolves. Place carrot and daikon in a glass container, pour the cooled liquid over, and chill at least 1 hour.
3. Preheat oven to 400°F. Pat pork dry, focusing on the skin. If keeping skin on, score with small cuts.
4. Roast pork skin-side up for 45 minutes at 400°F. Add a little water to the dish to prevent burning.
5. Reduce heat to 350°F. Slice pork into smaller pieces, toss, and roast another 15 minutes, until tender. Optional: broil at 500°F for a few minutes for crispy skin.
6. Lightly toast baguettes. Slice lengthwise, leaving a hinge.
7. Spread mayo and Sriracha on both halves.
8. Toss the herbs with the pork and jalapeño, with a tiny pinch of salt, sugar, and pepper.
9. Layer cucumber, then pork-and-herb mixture, then carrot-daikon slaw. Serve.
