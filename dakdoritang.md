# Dakdoritang (Korean Spicy Chicken Stew)

**Serves:** 4 · **Prep:** 10 min · **Cook:** 30 min

## Ingredients

- 2.5–3 lbs bone-in chicken parts
- 10 oz potatoes, cut in chunks
- 1 medium carrot, cut in chunks (optional)
- 1/2 large onion, cut in chunks
- 7–8 plump cloves garlic
- 2–3 thin slices fresh ginger
- 2 scallions, cut in 2-inch pieces
- 1 red chile pepper (optional)

### Sauce

- 2–3 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chile flakes)
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 4 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp rice wine or mirin
- 2 tbsp gochujang
- Pinch of pepper
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp sesame seeds

## Instructions

### Stovetop

1. Combine 1.5 cups water, the chicken, and the sauce (hold back sesame oil and seeds) in a large pot. Stir well. Bring to a boil over high heat.
2. Reduce to medium and cook, covered, 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
3. Stir in the vegetables (except scallions). Cover and cook 10 minutes more.
4. Uncover and cook another 5 minutes, until chicken is tender and sauce has thickened. Stir in scallions, optional chile, sesame oil, and sesame seeds just before serving.

### Slow cooker

1. Place chicken, vegetables (except scallions), and all sauce ingredients (except sesame oil and seeds) in the slow cooker. Stir well.
2. Cook on high for 4 hours, or low for 6 hours.
3. Stir in scallions, sesame oil, and sesame seeds. Skim excess fat if desired.
