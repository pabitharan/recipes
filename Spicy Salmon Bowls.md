# Spicy Salmon Bowls

**Serves:** 4 · **Prep:** 20 min · **Total:** 1 hr

## Ingredients

### Salmon

- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/3 cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 1/3 cup reduced-sodium soy sauce
- 1/4 cup chili garlic sauce
- Juice of 1 lime
- 2 tbsp honey
- 4 (4-oz) salmon fillets

### Quick-pickled cucumbers

- 1/2 cup rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp granulated sugar
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 2 tsp toasted sesame oil
- 3 Persian cucumbers, thinly sliced

### Spicy mayo

- 1/2 cup mayonnaise
- 2 tbsp Sriracha
- 2 tsp toasted sesame oil

### Bowls

- 4 cups cooked brown rice
- 1 avocado, thinly sliced
- 1 medium carrot, finely grated
- 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
- Fresh cilantro, torn
- Toasted sesame seeds

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 350°F. Whisk garlic, soy sauce, olive oil, chili garlic sauce, lime juice, and honey. Add salmon and toss gently. Place on a foil-lined baking sheet.
2. Bake 20–25 minutes, until fork-tender.
3. **Pickled cucumbers:** Microwave vinegar, sugar, and salt in a heatproof bowl or jar until dissolved, about 2 minutes. Stir in sesame oil, add cucumbers, cover, and let sit at room temperature.
4. **Spicy mayo:** Stir together mayonnaise, Sriracha, and sesame oil.
5. Divide rice among bowls. Top with salmon, pickled cucumbers, avocado, carrot, onion, cilantro, and sesame seeds. Drizzle with spicy mayo.
